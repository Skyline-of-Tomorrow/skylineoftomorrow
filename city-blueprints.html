Chicago's Alternative Fuel Source Distribution

Six GIS maps depicting the distrubtion of Alternative Fuel Source (AFS) sites across Chicago. The first map compares the site location data with a distribution of income per capita throughout the Chicago area. The second and fifth maps both depict a Kernel Density Estimate for the density of the AFS site distribution. The third map depicts the distribution of AFS sites per their accessibility (private or public site). The fourth map zooms in on the Loop area of Chicago and depicts the density of AFS sites via a KDE analysis, where the varying zones are restricted to include 10%, 20%, 40%, 60%, and 80% of the total sites as you move outward from the Loop. The sixth and final map depicts a point in grid density analysis of AFS sites throughout Chicago. More can be read about these maps on the Re-inventing the Brick page of this site.

A Spatial Analysis of Alternative Fuel Sources in Chicago

August 13th, 2020

By: Matthew Javier Gaughan

Transitioning away from fossil fuels is integral to creating a more environmentally sustainable future. Assessing the location of alternative fuel sources in relation to various parts of the city could serve to better identify patterns emerging in who gets to participate in sustainable environmental practices. This report looks at the locations of alternative fuel sources throughout Chicago.
