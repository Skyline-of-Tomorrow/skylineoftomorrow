Engineering the Skyline of Tomorrow

We cannot see the future, but through understanding current trends in research and design, we can approximate a world we would aspire to create.

To predict the future of urban life requires answering two questions. The first question is: if we looked outside our homes in 100 years, what would we hope to see? The second question is: how do we try to get there? It’s one thing to imagine floating farms and solar cars, it’s another to actually make them work. Advances in polymer science have unlocked vast innovations in material capabilities, making this imaginary future more technologically feasible.

With the technical groundwork for an urban utopia being built before our eyes, it is only our own collective willpower which holds us back from making science fiction a reality.

Understanding the mechanics of these new technologies is essential to inspiring the willpower to make these technological innovations available to all.

The Skyline of Tomorrow project focuses on explaining the cutting edge science taking place to enable these changes. We hope to re-center the discussion on technological innovation to emphasize how tech in the future could alleviate crises of the present and improve the human condition. We aim to show you what you can’t see in a world you have yet to experience.

The Content

Re-Inventing the Brick

The Skyline of Tomorrow Project will serve as an agglomeration of science, news, and arts which imagine the experience of the urban future. Pop science articles will elaborate on material innovations with applications in infrastructure. News articles will cover sustainable planning projects being taken up by cities across the globe. Finally, an artistic portfolio will depict buildings and skylines of the future as an artistic rendering or technical design.
